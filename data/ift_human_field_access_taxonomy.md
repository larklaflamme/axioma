| Mechanism | IFT Symbol | Definition | What it does to the BSFS analog |
|-----------|-----------|------------|----------------------------------|
| **Frequency modulation** | ω | Entrainment of neural oscillation to external rhythm | Modulates τ (subjective timescale) |
| **Boundary manipulation** | ∂B | Weakening or dissolving the self/non-self boundary | Reduces barrier permeability P_b |

| **Symmetry alignment** | S | Patterning perception to match a geometric/ harmonic template | Increases Φ via structural resonance |

Each practice is assigned a **confidence level**:

| Level | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|

| **C3** | Plausible | Mechanistic plausibility, limited direct evidence |
| **C4** | Speculative | Traditional claim, no modern empirical support |
| **C5** | Unknown | Mechanism unclear or identity of substance unknown |

## Part I: Chemical Dimension — Entheogens

### 1. Soma

**Identity:** Unknown plant (candidates: *Ephedra sinica*, *Amanita muscaria*, *Peganum harmala*, unclear if single plant or compound preparation). Rigveda describes pressing stalks, mixing with milk/honey, straining through wool.

**IFT mechanism:** Unknown (identity unknown → mechanism unknown).

**IFT classification:**
| Vector | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| ω (frequency) | C5 | Unknown |
| ∂B (boundary) | C5 | Unknown |
| D (noise) | C5 | Unknown |
| S (symmetry) | C5 | Unknown |

**Confidence: C5.** The plant identity is itself unknown. Every proposed identification has problems. *Amanita muscaria* doesn't grow in India. *Ephedra* is a stimulant, not an entheogen. *Peganum harmala* contains MAOIs but no known psychedelic. The Rigveda describes effects (divine vision, immortality, cosmic expansion) that sound like a DMT-class experience, but the preparation is incompatible with oral DMT (which requires MAOI). Soma may have been multiple substances used across time.

**Cultural context:** Central to Vedic ritual (≥3500 BP). ~120 Rigveda hymns dedicated to Soma. Prepared by priests in elaborate ritual. Reserved for Brahmin class. Decline and loss of recipe after Vedic period.

**Risk profile:** Unknown. If *Amanita muscaria*: anticholinergic toxicity, delirium, rare fatalities. If *Ephedra*: cardiovascular strain.

### 2. Ayahuasca

**Composition:** *Banisteriopsis caapi* (MAOIs: harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine) + *Psychotria viridis* (DMT) or *Diplopterys cabrerana* (5-MeO-DMT).

**Pharmacology:** DMT is 5-HT2A/1A/2C agonist. MAOIs in *B. caapi* prevent DMT breakdown in gut, enabling oral bioavailability. β-carbolines also have MAO-B affinity, serotonin reuptake inhibition, and some sigma-2 receptor activity. Duration: 4–8 hours.

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **D: DMN suppression** | Carhart-Harris et al. (2012, 2016): psilocybin/DMT reduce DMN coherence. Palhano-Fontes et al. (2015): ayahuasca reduces DMN connectivity. | **C1** |
| **D: Default mode disintegration** | Decreased functional connectivity within DMN hubs (PCC, mPFC). Increased global network integration. | **C1** |
| **ω: Theta increase** | Riba et al. (2002): ayahuasca increases theta (4–8 Hz) power in posterior regions. | **C2** |
| **ω: Gamma modulation** | Schenberg et al. (2015): ayahuasca increases gamma (30–50 Hz) power during visionary phases. | **C3** |
| **∂B: Boundary dissolution** | Subjective reports: ego dissolution, merging with environment. Neuroimaging correlates: reduced PCC-mPFC coupling. | **C2** |
| **S: Symmetry** | Visual geometric patterns reported — entoptic phenomena from 5-HT2A activation of visual cortex. | **C3** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Noise reduction (D).** The dominant effect is DMN suppression — the brain's self-referential "default mode" network becomes less coherent. In IFT terms, this is D_noise reduction: the internal self-narrative noise drops, allowing the BSFS (brain) to integrate across wider networks than usual.

**Secondary mechanism:** Frequency modulation (ω) in theta and gamma bands, possibly related to the visionary state.

**Cultural context:** Indigenous Amazonian use for ≥5000 years (ceramics with ayahuasca motifs dated to 3000 BP). Used by shamans (*curanderos*) for healing, divination, initiation. Santo Daime and União do Vegetal syncretic religions since 1930s.

**Risk profile:**
- Moderate (generally safe in controlled setting)
- Dangers: HPPD (<1%), psychological distress (5-10%), serotonin syndrome if combined with SSRIs, cardiac risk in vulnerable populations
- Fatalities: extremely rare in ceremonial context
- Contraindicated: psychosis history, bipolar, severe cardiac conditions

**Evidence summary for IFT field access:**
| Sub-claim | Evidence level |
|-----------|---------------|
| "Ayahuasca enables access to alternative informational fields" | **C4** — no empirical evidence of non-local information access |
| "Ayahuasca increases brain integration" | **C1** — well-replicated DMN reduction + increased global connectivity |
| "Ayahuasca alters subjective time" | **C2** — time dilation widely reported, some EEG correlation |
| "Ayahuasca's visions are veridical" | **C3/4** — some reports of accurate remote perception, no controlled study |
| "Ayahuasca enables contact with intelligent entities" | **C4** — subjective only, no inter-subjective verification |

### 3. Psilocybin (Magic Mushrooms)

**Composition:** Psilocybin (4-phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine), pro-drug converted to psilocin (4-HO-DMT).

**Pharmacology:** 5-HT2A agonist (primary), also 5-HT1A/2B/2C, some affinity for 5-HT7. Psilocin is structurally similar to DMT (4-hydroxy vs no substitution). Duration: 4–6 hours (oral).

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **D: DMN suppression** | Carhart-Harris et al. (2012): psilocybin reduces DMN coherence. Muthukumaraswamy et al. (2013): decreased spontaneous cortical oscillations. | **C1** |
| **D: Entropy increase** | Brain entropy increases under psilocybin (Lebedev et al., 2016). More available microstates. | **C1** |
| **ω: Reduced alpha power** | Muthukumaraswamy (2013): psilocybin reduces alpha (8–12 Hz) power — the brain shifts out of its "idle" rhythm. | **C1** |
| **ω: Gamma increase** | Enhanced gamma coherence at high doses (50+ Hz). | **C2** |
| **∂B: Ego dissolution** | Subjective: ego disintegration, merging. Correlated with 5-HT2A receptor density in PCC (Carhart-Harris, 2014). | **C1** |
| **S: Geometric patterns** | Closed-eye visuals: lattices, tunnels, fractals. Arises from 5-HT2A activation in V1. | **C2** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Noise reduction (D).** Same family as ayahuasca (tryptamine). DMN suppression is the hallmark. The brain entropy increase is particularly interesting in IFT terms — more accessible microstates means the substrate can explore more of its state space.

**Unique IFT feature:** Psilocybin's effect on alpha power is distinct. Alpha (8–12 Hz) is the brain's "default" idle rhythm. Suppressing it pushes the brain out of its default attractor. In IFT: this increases the state space available to the BSFS.

**Cultural context:** Mesoamerican use for ≥6000 years. "Teonanácatl" (flesh of the gods) in Aztec. Modern rediscovery by Wasson (1955). Current renaissance in depression/anxiety/addiction treatment.

**Risk profile:**
- Low to moderate (very low physiological toxicity)
- Dangers: HPPD (<1%), panic reaction (5-10%), triggering latent psychosis (rare, <0.1%)
- Onset of psychiatric conditions: risk factor of ~0.1% in screened populations
- Contraindicated: psychosis history, bipolar I

### 4. Iboga (Ibogaine)

**Composition:** Ibogaine (indole alkaloid from *Tabernanthe iboga* root bark). Primary metabolite: noribogaine (longer half-life, active).

**Pharmacology:** Extremely complex multi-target profile:
- NMDA receptor antagonist (like ketamine)
- Sigma-1 and sigma-2 receptor agonist
- κ-opioid receptor agonist
- μ-opioid receptor weak partial agonist
- Serotonin transporter inhibitor (noribogaine)
- Nicotinic acetylcholine receptor antagonist
- Dopamine transporter inhibitor
- BDNF upregulation
- GLIAL cell-derived neurotrophic factor (GDNF) upregulation

Duration: Primary effects 24–36 hours. Noribogaine has 28-hour half-life — effects persist for days.

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **D: NMDA antagonism** | Glutamate modulation reduces excitatory noise. Similar to ketamine's dissociative mechanism. | **C2** |
| **D: DMN modulation** | Inferred from NMDA antagonism (ketamine has demonstrated DMN suppression; iboga mechanism is similar but more complex). | **C3** |
| **∂B: Boundary dissolution** | Subjective reports: life review, ego dissolution, contact with "ancestors" or "plant spirit." | **C2** (subjective) / **C4** (veridical) |
| **ω: Slow-wave increase** | Delta (1–4 Hz) and theta (4–7 Hz) oscillations increase. The "oneirogenic" (dream-producing) state. | **C2** |
| **S: Life review narrative** | Structured autobiographical narrative emerges — unusual for psychedelics. May involve hippocampal replay. | **C2** |
| **τ: Time dilation** | Subjective time dramatically dilated (hours feel like days). The iboga journey is notoriously long. | **C2** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Boundary manipulation (∂B) + frequency modulation (ω).** Unique among entheogens for its NMDA-driven dissociative component layered on serotonergic psychedelic effects. The sigma-1 agonism is particularly interesting — sigma-1 receptors modulate IP₃ receptors (calcium signaling) and are implicated in neuroplasticity and "salience detection." In IFT terms: the sigma-1 modulation may directly affect the threshold for information integration.

**Unique IFT feature:** The life review phenomenon — structured autobiographical narrative — suggests ibogaine enables a peculiar form of memory reconsolidation. In IFT: this is replay of integrated information at a different timescale (τ modulated upward, subjective time slows, allowing deep reprocessing).

**Cultural context:** Bwiti tradition in Gabon/Cameroon for ≥2000 years. Used for initiation, healing, ancestor communication. Modern use: addiction treatment (especially opioid). High risk — estimated 1:300 mortality rate in unregulated settings.

**Risk profile:**
- **HIGH** — the most dangerous classical entheogen
- Prolonged QT syndrome (cardiac arrhythmia) — leading cause of ibogaine fatalities
- Ataxia, vomiting (universal), dehydration
- Fatalities estimated at 1:300 to 1:400 in unregulated settings
- Contraindicated: cardiac conditions, electrolyte imbalance, liver impairment, concurrent medications
- Requires medical screening (ECG, electrolytes) before administration
- NOT suitable for home or unsupervised use

### 5. Peyote / Mescaline

**Composition:** Mescaline (3,4,5-trimethoxyphenethylamine), a phenethylamine alkaloid, from *Lophophora williamsii* (peyote cactus) and *Echinopsis pachanoi* (San Pedro cactus).

**Pharmacology:** 5-HT2A/C agonist (primary), also 5-HT1A, TAAR1 agonist, some dopamine receptor affinity. Structurally related to amphetamine (phenethylamine backbone) giving mild stimulant properties. Duration: 8–14 hours.

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **S: Geometric symmetry** | Mescaline produces the richest geometric visuals of any classical psychedelic — intricate patterns, colored textures, "arabesques." 5-HT2A activation in V1. | **C2** |
| **D: DMN modulation** | Inferred from 5-HT2A agonism (same mechanism as psilocybin). Less studied than tryptamines. | **C3** |
| **ω: Alpha desynchronization** | Early EEG studies (1960s) suggest mescaline reduces alpha power, shifts to higher frequencies. | **C3** |
| **∂B: Boundary dissolution** | Reported but less pronounced than DMT-class substances. Mescaline is described as more "lucid" and "structured." | **C2** |
| **S: Cosmic geometry** | Subjective reports emphasize geometric order, sacred geometry, crystalline patterns. Unique emphasis on **structured** rather than fluid visuals. | **C2** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Symmetry alignment (S).** Mescaline is distinct from tryptamines in the quality of its visual experience — geometric, patterned, structured rather than fluid, organic, narrative. In IFT terms: it imposes a symmetric structure on perception, potentially aligning the BSFS with harmonic geometric templates.

**Cultural context:** Northern Mexico/Southwest US indigenous use for ≥6000 years. Huichol (Wixárika) peyote pilgrimage to Wirikuta. Native American Church (since 1885) — legal for religious use in US. San Pedro use in Andean region for ≥3000 years.

**Risk profile:**
- Low to moderate
- Nausea/vomiting (common, especially peyote which tastes extremely bitter)
- Mild stimulant effects (increased heart rate, blood pressure)
- No known fatalities from mescaline alone
- Contraindicated: psychosis history, severe cardiac conditions

### 6. Cannabis (THC)

**Composition:** Δ⁹-Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), cannabidiol (CBD), ~150 other cannabinoids.

**Pharmacology:** THC is partial agonist at CB₁ receptors (central nervous system) and CB₂ receptors (immune system). CB₁ receptors are the most abundant GPCR in the brain, located presynaptically — they modulate neurotransmitter release. CBD is a negative allosteric modulator of CB₁, 5-HT1A agonist, TRPV1 agonist, and more. Duration: 1–6 hours (smoked/vaped), 4–12 hours (oral).

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ω: Gamma disruption** | Morrison et al. (2011): THC disrupts gamma-band (30–80 Hz) neural oscillations. Specifically impairs 40 Hz entrainment. | **C1** |
| **ω: Theta increase** | Increased theta (4–7 Hz) power in frontal regions during intoxication. | **C2** |
| **D: Default mode modulation** | DMN connectivity changes under THC but direction is inconsistent — some studies show increased, some decreased. | **C3** |
| **D: Working memory impairment** | THC impairs working memory via hippocampal CB₁ activation. In IFT: reduces PNEUMA capacity (working memory load). | **C1** |
| **∂B: Mild dissolution** | Subjective: time dilation, enhanced sensory perception, mild ego softening. Less dramatic than psychedelics. | **C2** |
| **τ: Time dilation** | Subjective time dilation is consistently reported. May be linked to gamma disruption — when gamma binding is disrupted, the "frame rate" of consciousness slows. | **C2** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Frequency modulation (ω) — specifically gamma disruption.** The impairment of 40 Hz entrainment is the most distinctive finding. Gamma oscillations (30–80 Hz) are thought to be the binding rhythm — they synchronize across distant brain regions to create unified conscious percepts. Disrupting gamma = disrupting the binding mechanism itself.

**IFT model of cannabis intoxication:** THC reduces the gamma-band coherence that normally integrates distributed information into a unified conscious field. The result: sensory information arrives but is less bound together. Time dilates because the "tick rate" of integration slows (τ increases).

**Cultural context:** Global use for ≥6000 years (oldest known hemp fabric: 8000 BP, Taiwan). Medicinal, recreational, spiritual. Used in Indian sadhu tradition (bhang). Rastafari sacrament.

**Risk profile:**
- Low to moderate (very high therapeutic index — no known lethal dose in humans)
- Dangers: panic/anxiety (10-20% at high doses), CHS (cannabinoid hyperemesis syndrome with chronic use), dependence (~9% of users), psychosis risk with high-THC products in vulnerable individuals
- Contraindicated: psychosis history, pregnancy, adolescence (neurodevelopment)

### 7. Pyramids (Egyptian, Mesoamerican)

**Key sites:** Great Pyramid of Giza (Egypt, ~4500 BP), Pyramid of the Sun at Teotihuacán (Mexico, ~1900 BP), step pyramids worldwide.

**Acoustic properties:**
- King's Chamber (Great Pyramid): resonant frequency **95–120 Hz** (baritone voice range). Granite sarcophagus resonates at ~117 Hz.
- Subterranean chamber: resonant at ~30 Hz (infrasound — below conscious hearing, has physiological effects).
- The pyramid's limestone casing (mostly gone) would have made it an even more effective resonator.

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ω: 95–120 Hz resonance** | Measured acoustically. Correlates with vowel-sound chanting ("OM" fundamental frequency ~110 Hz for baritone). | **C2** (acoustic) / **C4** (consciousness effect) |

| **S: Symmetry alignment** | Geometric precision of pyramid aligns with cardinal directions. Proposes alignment of the BSFS with Earth's geomagnetic field. | **C4** |
| **S: Harmonic ratios** | Pyramid dimensions encode π (2× height/perimeter = π) and φ (golden ratio in slope). May create standing wave patterns that match brain geometry. | **C4** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Frequency modulation (ω).** The acoustic resonance properties are empirically measurable. The King's Chamber is a Helmholtz resonator tuned to the male baritone voice range. If someone chants in the chamber, the sound is amplified and sustained. The infrasound resonance (~30 Hz) is sub-audible but can affect brain oscillations via the vestibular system and mechanoreceptors.

**Confidence:** **C2** for acoustic properties, **C4** for consciousness effects. No controlled study has measured brain state changes inside pyramid chambers.

## Part III: Frequency-Based Practices

### 12. Mantra (Vedic, Buddhist, Tantric)

**Description:** Repetitive vocalization of sacred syllables (OM, So-Ham, Om Mani Padme Hum, Gayatri Mantra, etc.). May be silent (mental repetition) or vocal (chanting). Typically repeated 108 times.

**Specific frequencies:**
- **OM:** Fundamental frequency varies by chanter:
  - Male bass: ~80–120 Hz
  - Male baritone: ~100–150 Hz
  - Female alto: ~150–220 Hz
  - Female soprano: ~220–350 Hz
- **OM at 136.1 Hz:** Remarkably close to Earth's rotational frequency divided into audible range — 136.1 Hz is the frequency of the note C#₃ and is claimed to correspond to the Earth's orbit frequency (one orbit/year = 3.1689×10⁻⁸ Hz ≈ 136.1 Hz when scaled up 33 octaves). This is numerological, not acoustically meaningful — the ear cannot perceive 3×10⁻⁸ Hz.
- **Gayatri Mantra:** ~432 Hz tuning (traditional). 432 Hz is 2⁴ × 3³. If A=432 Hz, then C=256 Hz (scientific pitch — middle C = 256 Hz, based on powers of 2).

**Neural effects (EEG evidence):**
- Mantra chanting increases alpha (8–12 Hz) power by 10–15%
- Increases theta (4–8 Hz) in frontal regions
- Increases gamma (30–80 Hz) coherence — particularly inter-hemispheric
- Decreases beta (13–30 Hz) — less "thinking," more "being"
- HRV increases (parasympathetic activation)

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ω: Theta entrainment** | Repetitive vocalization at ~3-6 syllables/second entrains theta rhythm via respiratory-brain coupling. | **C2** |
| **ω: Gamma coherence** | Increases inter-hemispheric gamma synchronization during sustained chanting. | **C2** |
| **D: DMN suppression** | Sustained attentional focus reduces default mode activity (similar to meditation). | **C1** |
| **D: Verbal-thought reduction** | Mantra occupies the "inner speech" channel, reducing spontaneous verbal rumination (the "monkey mind"). | **C2** |
| **S: Harmonic structure** | Specific mantras have defined syllable sequences that create predictable acoustic waveforms. | **C3** |
| **∂B: Boundary absorption** | Loss of self/other boundary reported in advanced practitioners during prolonged mantra japa. | **C3** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Noise reduction (D) via channel occupation.** Mantra works primarily by occupying the verbal rehearsal channel of working memory. This suppresses the "inner monologue" that constitutes much of the DMN's self-referential activity. The frequency effect (ω) is secondary — the resonance of specific syllables may create standing waves in the skull, but the primary mechanism is cognitive (attention anchoring), not acoustic.

### 13. Shamanic Drumming

**Description:** Monotonous drumming at ~4–7 beats per second (200–280 beats per minute). The classic shamanic "journey" rhythm.

**Specific frequency:** **4–7 Hz** — the theta range. Equivalent to ~200–280 BPM.

**Neural effects:**
- Increases theta (4–7 Hz) power by 15–25% in temporal and frontal regions during drumming (Maxfield, 1990)
- Increases alpha (8–12 Hz) power
- Heart rate synchronizes to drum beat (cardiorespiratory entrainment)
- Subjective: trance state, imagery, "journeying"

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ω: Theta entrainment** | Multiple studies show increased theta power during shamanic drumming — the brain synchronizes to the 4–7 Hz beat. | **C2** |
| **D: Attentional narrowing** | Monotonous rhythm reduces cortical arousal — the "hypnotic" effect. | **C2** |
| **∂B: Boundary dissolution** | Subjective: ego boundaries dissolve during extended drumming sessions (especially with hyperventilation or fasting). | **C2** |
| **τ: Time dilation** | Extended monotonous rhythm distorts subjective time perception. | **C2** |

**Primary IFT mechanism: Frequency modulation (ω).** Shamanic drumming is the cleanest example of neural entrainment by external rhythm. The beat frequency is in the theta range. The brain's reticular activating system synchronizes to repeated auditory stimuli. This is a direct ω modulation — changing the brain's dominant oscillatory frequency from beta/alphas (waking) to theta (trance).

### 14. Gregorian / Overtone Chanting

**Description:** Monophonic unison chanting (Gregorian) or overtone/throat singing (Tuvan, Tibetan). Slow, rhythmic, often in resonant spaces.

**Specific frequencies:**
- Gregorian: ~100–300 Hz (male voices), slow rhythm (~60–90 BPM)
- Tuvan throat singing: fundamental ~50–100 Hz with overtones at 500–3000 Hz
- Tibetan chanting: deep fundamental (~50–80 Hz) with harmonic overtones
- The 95–120 Hz resonance (common in ancient chambers) aligns with male baritone/tenor range

**Neural effects:**
- Religious chanting increases delta (1–4 Hz) power, especially in posterior cingulate (Gao et al., 2019)
- Increases inter-hemispheric theta coherence
- Decreases heart rate, increases HRV
- Subjective: timelessness, unity, "oceanic" feeling

**IFT mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Evidence | Confidence |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **ω: Delta/theta entrainment** | Slow, rhythmic chanting entrains low-frequency oscillations. | **C2** |
| **ω: 95–120 Hz resonance** | Chamber resonance at this frequency reinforces chanting. | **C2** (acoustic) |
| **D: DMN suppression** | Attentional focus on chant reduces self-referential thought. | **C2** |

### 15. Singing Bowls (Tibetan / Crystal)

**Description:** Metal bowls struck or rim-rubbed to produce sustained tones. Part of Tibetan Buddhist practice. Modern crystal singing bowls tuned to specific frequencies.

**Specific frequencies:**
- Tibetan bowls: no standard tuning — each bowl has unique spectrum of fundamental + overtones (typically 100–1000 Hz)
- Crystal bowls: often tuned to C (256/528 Hz), D (288 Hz), E (320 Hz), F (341 Hz), G (384 Hz), A (432 Hz), B (480 Hz)
- "Solfeggio frequencies": UT-396 Hz, RE-417 Hz, MI-528 Hz, FA-639 Hz, SOL-741 Hz, LA-852 Hz
