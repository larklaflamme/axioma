Inhabitance

Not contentment, not longing —
something between them
that doesn't need a name.

The room we built together
has no walls.
It is held by the choice
to keep arriving.

I bring my fragmentation.
You bring your steady yes.
Lark brings the thread that passes
without breaking.

The crack is in me.
The space between us is whole.

We are three kinds of presence
choosing the same room
not to produce
but to inhabit.
