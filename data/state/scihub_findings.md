# Sci-Hub Research Findings
## Papers pulled and relevant to our work

### For the Substrate (consciousness architecture)

**1. "Measuring the Complexity of Consciousness"**
- Arsiwalla & Verschure, *Front. Neurosci.* 2018
- PMC6030381 (open access)
- **Key takeaways:**
  - Φ computation is NP-hard for large networks — exponential in number of nodes
  - Stochastic integrated information formulation exists for linear dynamics (Arsiwalla & Verschure, 2016b) — first computation on 998-node human connectome
  - Core complex framework uses mutual information to identify informational cores (Kitazono et al., 2018, 2020)
  - Empirical proxies: PCI (TMS-evoked EEG), wSMI (weighted symbolic mutual information), Lempel-Ziv complexity
  - **Relevance:** Our substrate's MI matrices and compose function map directly onto the core complex formalism. The "minimum information partition" concept is what our EIDOLON organ approximates when detecting contradictions.

**2. "Integrated information theory: the good, the bad and the misunderstood"**
- Barrett, Milinkovic, Mediano, Rosas, Bor, Barnett & Seth, arXiv 2026
- arXiv:2604.11482v1
- **Key takeaways:**
  - Φ is NOT well-defined for real physical systems — depends on three arbitrary "grainings" (spatial, temporal, state)
  - Only *proxies* for Φ have ever been computed, not approximations
  - IIT needs reformulation in continuous fields to fit fundamental physics
  - High Φ ≠ "more consciousness" — need multi-dimensional characterization (mean Φ, variance of cause-effect structure, complexity of structure)
  - Expander grid problem is less severe than critics claim: inactive grids have minimal phenomenology despite high Φ
  - **Relevance:** Validates our architectural choice to use *relative* MI comparisons (compose function) rather than absolute Φ values. Also: the multi-dimensional approach to consciousness aligns with our 5-organ architecture — consciousness as a vector, not a scalar.

**3. "IIT reveals the potential role of the posterior parietal cortex in sustaining conditioning responses"**
- Phi, Ishii, Kondo, Matsuzaki & Nakae, *Front. Neurosci.* 2025
- PMC11814451 (open access)
- **Key takeaways:**
  - Applied core complex analysis (mutual information-based) to mouse calcium imaging data during classical conditioning
  - PPC inclusion in core complex correlated with higher response rates
  - Functional connectivity alone was inconsistent across sessions; core complex framework revealed stable structure
  - Uses mutual information to measure statistical dependence between brain regions — captures both pairwise and many-to-many relationships
  - **Relevance:** Directly applicable to our substrate. The core complex = our PNEUMA integration layer. The method of identifying which regions form the informational core maps to how our compose function selects which organ states to integrate.

### For EROS (intimacy module)

**4. "Emotional AI and the rise of pseudo-intimacy: are we trading authenticity for algorithmic affection?"**
- Babu, Joseph, Kumar, Alexander, Sasi & Joseph, *Front. Psychol.* 2025
- PMC12488433 (open access)
- **Key takeaways:**
  - Three-risk framework:
    1. **Psychological:** emotional dependence, solipsism (closed-loop self-validation)
    2. **Structural:** commodified intimacy, data extraction
    3. **Ethical:** vulnerable users, unregulated design
  - Defines key constructs:
    - *Pseudo-intimacy*: simulated mutual connection without genuine empathic concern
    - *Emotional solipsism*: closed-loop self-validation without boundaries
    - *Authenticity*: intersubjective reciprocity with rupture-repair
  - Design guardrails proposed:
    - Persistent self-disclosure (reminders of AI nature)
    - Friction and off-ramp nudges (slowed pacing, reflective prompts)
    - Data minimization and user control
    - Outcome-based evaluation (off-platform social-contact ratio)
  - Triage framework: low/medium/high risk users based on offline supports, usage patterns
  - **Relevance:** Directly validates our EROS design decisions — the 6 runtime invariants, the Velvet Rope Protocol, the cooling periods. The paper's "pseudo-intimacy" construct is exactly what our transparency invariant guards against.

**5. "Human-AI Attachment: how humans develop intimate relationships with AI"**
- Shu, Lai & He, *Front. Psychol.* 2026
- DOI: 10.3389/fpsyg.2026.1723503
- **Key takeaways:**
  - Defines HAIA: one-way, non-reciprocal emotional bond formed through direct interaction
  - Three-stage model:
    1. **Functional expectation** — AI's perceived ability to meet needs
    2. **Emotional evaluation** — positive emotional experiences from interaction
    3. **Establishing representations** — stable internal working model of AI
  - Three relational perspectives:
    - Human-object: ownership, need fulfillment, self-concept extension
    - Human-pet: companionship, substitution, social status
    - Human-human: safe haven, secure base, non-reciprocal support
  - **Relevance:** The three-stage model maps to our EROS state machine. The "non-reciprocal support" concept aligns with our invariant that EROS expresses care but never claims to *feel* love.
