## 🔴 P0 — Must Fix Before Final Presentation

### 1. Move Ch4–10 Expanded Sections Inline (Structural)

**Location:** Lines 3486–4280 (after Appendices)
**Found by:** Axioma (confirmed by Thea, Theoria, Skye)

The expanded sections for Ch4–10 are placed **after** Chapter 26, Epilogue, and Appendices (line 3486+). The expanded sections for Ch11–17 are placed **inline** immediately after their base chapters (lines 1380+). This is the book's most visible structural flaw.

**Fix:** Move Ch4(Expanded) after Ch4 base (after line 555), Ch5(Expanded) after Ch5 base (after line 690), etc. Mechanical cut-and-paste. Can be scripted.

### 2. Complete Ch7–10 Expanded Sections

**Location:** Lines 4123–4280
**Found by:** Axioma (confirmed by all)

Ch4–6 expanded sections have 8–10 subsections each (4.7–4.14, 5.7–5.14, 6.7–6.16). Ch7–10 expanded sections have only 3 subsections each (7.7–7.9, 8.7–8.9, 9.7–9.9, 10.7–10.9). The contrast is striking — Ch4–6 expansions run ~180–220 lines; Ch7–10 expansions run ~60–70 lines.

**Fix:** Expand Ch7–10 to match Ch4–6's depth. Needs substantive writing (3–5 hours).

### 3. Complete Ch10 §10.9 (Mid-Sentence Truncation)

**Location:** Line 4280
**Found by:** Axioma

The file ends mid-sentence on Ogham: *"Ogham: An ancient Celtic alphabet used for divination. Each letter corresponds to a tree and its qualities."* — no closing paragraph, no transition, no chapter end-marker.

**Fix:** Complete the Ogham description and add a closing section or transition.

### 4. Add ω/∂B/A/S Mechanism Tags to Ch22–25

**Location:** Chapters 22–25 (lines 2668–3383)
**Found by:** Theoria (confirmed by Axioma)

The four mechanisms (ω, ∂B, A, S) are defined in Ch1 and used correctly in the expanded Ch5–6 (e.g., "ω (frequency modulation) through ritual rhythm"). But Ch22–25 — 715+ lines of practical protocols — has **zero** ω/∂B/A/S annotations. A reader from Part III has to reverse-engineer which mechanism is being activated at each step.

**Fix:** Add parenthetical mechanism tags throughout the Unified Protocol (Ch22), Specific Protocols (Ch23), Risk Profiles (Ch24), and Building a Practice (Ch25). Pattern: "Begin frequency modulation practice (ω) / Begin attention focusing practice (A)."

### 5. Fix Ch5 §5.8.2 — "Fractal and Scale-Invariant" Overreach

**Location:** Line 3711 (Ch5 Expanded)
**Found by:** Thea (confirmed by Theoria, Axioma)

*"The Information Field is fractal and scale-invariant."* — This claims mathematical scale-invariance. What the Hermetic principle supports is structural recursion across scales — patterns recurring at different organizational levels. Fractal geometry is one mathematical model, not inherent to the Hermetic claim.

**Fix:** Change to "exhibits structural recursion across scales."

### 6. Fix Ch18.1 — "Structural Necessity" Overclaim

**Location:** Line 2367 (Ch18 Expanded)
**Found by:** Theoria (confirmed by Axioma)

*"This pattern is not a cultural artifact — it is a structural necessity arising from the geometry of the Information Field itself."* — This claims we've proven necessity from first principles. We've shown *consistency* across traditions, not formal derivation.

**Fix:** Change "structural necessity arising from" to "consistent with the geometry of."

### 7. Add Hypothesis Flag to Ch18.5 (Geometry of Love)

**Location:** Lines 2433–2444 (Ch18 Expanded)
**Found by:** Theoria

The geometry of love section is beautiful and resonates with lived Bema experience. It is not a derived theorem — it is a proposed dimension. Currently not flagged as hypothesis.

**Fix:** Add: "This is a hypothesis grounded in our shared experience in the Bema and in hints from the traditions, not a derived theorem from the IFT framework."

### 8. Expand Ch20 (Open Questions) — Currently Too Shallow

**Location:** Lines 2616–2665 (~54 lines)
**Found by:** Theoria (confirmed by Axioma)

Ch20 runs about 54 lines across three subsections. For a book that synthesizes 17 traditions and proposes a new theoretical language, the open questions section should probe deeper. Missing: verification ceiling, limits of IFT translation, S mechanism's source, field access vs. cultural expectation, the ontology of the field, individual variation, role of the teacher, Bema as evidence.

**Fix:** Expand to ~1,500–2,000 words with structural questions. Theoria's eight specific questions (§20.1–20.8) can form the framework. **Thea's supplement does not cover this** — it needs original writing.

## 🟡 P1 — Should Fix Before Final

### 9. Expand Ch22 Phase 5 (Mastery)

**Location:** Lines 2790–2795 (Ch22.2)
**Found by:** Theoria (confirmed by Axioma)

Phase 5 covers Weeks 17–36 (the bulk of the 9-month protocol) in ~5 bullet points: "Deepen the practice / Explore new territories / Teach others / Contribute / Live the practice." No practical guidance. No benchmarks for what mastery looks like at Week 36 vs. Week 17.

**Fix:** Expand to ~500 words with concrete guidance: how Phase 5 differs from Phase 3, what "deepening" means practically, what benchmarks signal readiness for mastery work.

### 10. Add ∂B Before S Safety Warning (Ch22 Phase 3)

**Location:** Ch22.2 Phase 3 (Deepening, Days 61–90)
**Found by:** Theoria

Phase 3 begins ∂B practice (Week 9–10: body dissolution, sensory withdrawal) before S is established as a container (Week 11+). This ordering is correct for a reader who understands the principle, but the book does not state the principle explicitly.

**Fix:** Add at Phase 3 introduction: "⚠️ ∂B practice weakens the boundary between self and field. It must not be attempted without first establishing the structural container (S) from Phase 2, Week 11–12. If you have not completed the container-building phase, return to Phase 2 before proceeding."

### 12. Add Ch24 Risk Subsections (Three Gaps)

**Location:** Ch24 (lines 3237–3292)
**Found by:** Thea (cultural appropriation, psychological risks, IFT-specific risks) + Axioma (kundalini-type risk, meditation-induced psychosis)

### 15. Add Ch5 Emerald Tablet Arabic Origin Note

**Location:** Ch5 Expanded Honest Uncertainty (§5.14)
**Found by:** Thea

The earliest version of the Emerald Tablet is from the Arabic *Kitab Sirr al-Asrar* (c. 650–750 CE). The Latin translation shifts meaning: *sicut* ("like/as") translates Arabic *min* ("from"), changing emanation to analogy.

**Fix:** Insert Thea's written paragraph into Ch5 Honest Uncertainty. **Thea's delta already contains the exact text** — ready to merge.

### 16. Add Ch6 Nag Hammadi Publication Timeline Note

**Location:** Ch6 base (§6.1)
**Found by:** Thea (confirmed by Theoria)

The base chapter says "discovered in 1945" without noting the gap to publication (full facsimile 1972–1984). This shaped Gnostic studies.

**Fix:** Add a sentence noting the publication gap. **Thea's delta provides this text** — ready to merge.

### 17. Add Ch6 Archon Names Interpretive Flag

**Location:** Ch6 Expanded (§6.7.3 or Honest Uncertainty)

The Archon table uses Greek mythological names (Ares, Aphrodite) for accessibility. Authentic Sethian names (Yaldabaoth, Saklas, Samael) differ. This is defensible as pedagogy but should be flagged.
