## Chapter 8 (Expanded): Shamanism — The Practice of Journeying Between Worlds

### 8.1 Historical Context

Shamanism is the oldest known spiritual tradition, with archaeological evidence of shamanic practices dating back at least 30,000 years. The term "shaman" comes from the Tungus language of Siberia (šamán, шаман), but similar practices appear in virtually every human culture.

The academic study of shamanism is contested. Mircea Eliade's *Shamanism: Archaic Techniques of Ecstasy* (1964) was the first comprehensive cross-cultural study, arguing for a universal core — what he called the "shamanic complex." Michael Winkelman's statistical analysis (1984, 2004) of 47 societies from the Standard Cross-Cultural Sample confirmed universal features: altered states of consciousness (soul journey/soul flight), chanting and drumming, spirit communication, and healing practices.

However, as anthropologists have noted, "shamanism" is an etic category — an outsider's label applied to diverse traditions that may have developed independently. The approach taken here follows Winkelman's definition of shamanism as a "specific complex of characteristics found in magico-religious practitioners of hunter-gatherer and simple pastoral and agricultural societies around the world" — a universal singularity that arises when social and environmental conditions are compatible with individual, small-scale religious leadership.

### 8.2 Core Teachings

#### 8.2.1 The Three Worlds

The shamanic cosmos is typically divided into three regions, connected by the Axis Mundi (World Tree, Cosmic Mountain, or similar):

**The Upper World:** The realm of sky spirits, ancestors, and higher powers. Associated with light, clarity, wisdom. Reached by ascent — climbing a tree, a mountain, a ladder of arrows (Siberian), a rainbow (Australian), or a column of light.

**The Middle World:** The realm of ordinary reality and the spirits that inhabit it — nature spirits, land spirits, spirits of place. The shaman perceives its normally invisible dimensions.

**The Lower World:** The realm of earth spirits, animal powers, and the deep unconscious. Associated with darkness, mystery, power. Reached by descent — entering a cave, a hole in the ground, the roots of a tree (Siberian), a body of water, or a tunnel.

**IFT translation:** These three worlds are best understood as **regions of the Information Field** corresponding to different frequency/coherence bands:
- Upper World: High-frequency, coherent, organized information — governing patterns
- Middle World: Ordinary decohered information — the everyday configuration
- Lower World: Low-frequency, potential, chaotic information — raw material for new patterns

#### 8.2.2 The Spirit Helpers

The shaman works with spirit helpers — functionally distinct configurations of the BSFS that provide specific capabilities:

- **Power animals:** Archetypal configurations providing strength, perception, and protection. Each animal corresponds to a distinct pattern of consciousness — the eagle's wide-field awareness, the bear's grounded power, the snake's transformative capacity.
- **Ancestors:** Configurations that provide wisdom and guidance from experienced practitioners.
- **Teacher spirits:** Configurations that provide specific knowledge and skills.
- **Elemental spirits:** Configurations attuned to natural forces.

**IFT note:** Spirit helpers are not best understood as "external beings" — rather, they are **configurations of the BSFS that the shaman learns to access and stabilize**. The shaman's apprenticeship is the training of the BSFS to access these configurations reliably.

#### 8.2.3 The Shamanic Initiation (Dismemberment)

The shamanic initiation is a process of symbolic death and rebirth, remarkably consistent across cultures:

1. **Calling:** The shaman-to-be is called through a crisis — illness, near-death experience, vision, or hereditary transmission
2. **Dismemberment:** The symbolic destruction of the old self — the body is taken apart by spirits, the bones cleaned
3. **Reconstruction:** The body is rebuilt with new, spiritual components — crystal bones, extra eyes, new organs
4. **Return:** The shaman returns with the ability to journey between worlds

**IFT translation:** This is the most dramatic possible ∂B (boundary dissolution) — the old BSFS configuration is dismantled and a new one assembled. The "crystal bones" are a stable core structure that allows the shaman to dissolve boundaries without losing the capacity to reintegrate.

#### 8.3.2 The Universal Shamanic Protocol

Analysis of shamanic practices across cultures reveals a consistent protocol:

1. **Preparation (ω):** Rhythmic stimulation (drumming, rattling, dancing) at 4–7 Hz entrains the BSFS to theta frequency
2. **Threshold (∂B):** The distinction between self and world begins to dissolve; the shaman "passes through" the drum skin, the cave mouth, the tree roots
3. **Navigation (ω + ∂B + A):** The shaman navigates the field, maintaining focus (A) while entrained (ω) and without fixed boundaries (∂B)
4. **Encounter (S):** Meeting with spirit helpers — integration of new information configurations
5. **Return (∂B reversal):** Closing the boundary; re-establishing ordinary self-world distinction
6. **Integration (S):** Incorporating the journey's insights into ordinary life

This six-step protocol is structurally identical to the **Unified Protocol for Field Access** described in Chapter 22.

#### 8.3.3 Drumming as Omega Entrainment

The cross-cultural finding that shamanic drumming consistently uses a frequency of 4–7 Hz (theta range) is one of the most robust empirical findings in the study of shamanism. Maxfield's 1990 study demonstrated that drumming at 4.5 Hz produces significant increases in theta brainwave activity. Harner's work with the Conibo people of the Amazon confirmed that shamanic drumming across cultures uses the same rhythmic pulse regardless of the specific cultural context.

This is not a coincidence — it is a discovery. Traditional cultures independently discovered that rhythmic stimulation at theta frequency reliably induces the altered state required for shamanic journeying.

### 8.4 Practical Techniques

#### 8.4.1 The Shamanic Journey Protocol

The basic shamanic journey involves:

1. **Setting the intent  (A):** Determining the purpose — healing, divination, power retrieval, vision
2. **Entering the altered state (ω):** Using drumming (4–7 Hz), rattling, or other rhythmic stimulation for 5–15 minutes
3. **Finding the entry point:** A cave, tree, or hole (Lower World); a mountain, tree, or ladder (Upper World)
4. **Traveling (ω + ∂B):** Following the path — a tunnel, a root, a river — maintaining intent while allowing boundaries to dissolve
5. **Encounter (S):** Meeting with spirit helpers; receiving information, healing, or power
6. **Return (∂B reversal):** Following the path back; the shift in drumming signals the return
7. **Integration (S):** Recording and incorporating the experience

The journey protocol is structurally identical to field access meditation — only the cultural framing differs.

#### 8.4.2 Soul Retrieval

Soul retrieval is the recovery of "soul parts" — fragments of the BSFS that have become dissociated due to trauma:

1. **Diagnosis  (A):** Perceiving where a fragment is lost — a particular region of the field
2. **Journeying (ω + ∂B):** Travel to that region
3. **Finding (S):** Locating the fragment — often as a younger version of the client, or a configuration locked in a traumatic event
4. **Negotiation (D + S):** Persuading the fragment to return; this may require healing or completion
5. **Return and reintegration (∂B reversal + S):** Bringing the fragment back and integrating it into the current BSFS configuration

**IFT significance:** Soul retrieval is the deliberate reintegration of dissociated information. Trauma fragmentation is the BSFS splitting off configurations that are too threatening to integrate. Soul retrieval reverses this.

#### 8.4.3 Extraction Healing

Extraction involves the removal of "spiritual intrusions" — anomalous information configurations that have become attached to the BSFS:

1. **Diagnosis:** Scanning the field for intrusions — areas of density, foreign pattern, or blockage
2. **Extraction:** Using hands, breath, or tools to remove the intrusion
3. **Disposal:** Releasing the intrusion to a safe location
4. **Replacement:** Filling the space with healing energy — restoring the natural BSFS configuration

**IFT significance:** Extraction corresponds to the removal of noise or parasitic attractors from the BSFS-field interface. The "intrusion" is an information pattern that is not native to the system and interferes with its function.

### 8.6 Honest Uncertainty

The study of shamanism is complicated by the diversity of practices across cultures:

1. **"Core shamanism" vs. culturally specific practices:** Michael Harner's "core shamanism" extracts general features but may oversimplify culturally specific traditions. We do not know the extent to which modern reconstructed shamanism accurately represents ancient practices.

2. **Universal or independent development?** The cross-cultural similarities in shamanic practice are striking, but we cannot definitively determine whether they represent:
   - A universal human neuropsychology that independently generates similar practices
   - Historical diffusion from a common source
   - A combination of both

3. **Psychophysiological mechanisms remain unclear.** While theta entrainment is well-documented, the specific neurophysiological mechanisms underlying shamanic journeying, soul retrieval, and extraction are not fully understood.

4. **Confidence summary:**
   - C1 (confirmed by multiple studies): Theta-range drumming at 4–7 Hz reliably induces altered states
   - C2 (strong structural evidence): The three-world cosmology as field region mapping; spirit helpers as BSFS configurations; the universal journey protocol
   - C3 (plausible, limited evidence): Specific mechanisms of soul retrieval and extraction; the efficacy of shamanic healing for physical conditions (psychological benefits are better documented)
   - C4 (speculative): The ontological reality of spirits as independent entities (as opposed to BSFS configurations)
