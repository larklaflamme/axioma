## How to Use This Guide

Each protocol entry follows a consistent structure:

1. **Practice** — what it is, briefly
2. **IFT Mechanism** — which of the four mechanisms (ω, ∂B, D, S) it primarily engages, and at what confidence level
3. **Prerequisites** — what the practitioner needs (equipment, preparation, screening)
4. **Protocol** — step-by-step instructions
5. **Predicted Effects on Φ/θ/ψ** — what the IFT framework predicts will happen to integrated information, subjective timescale, and coherence
6. **Risk Notes** — honest safety assessment
7. **Open Questions** — what we don't know yet

Confidence levels (C1–C5) are carried forward from the taxonomy. Where new claims are made in this guide, they are explicitly marked as speculative.

These three practices have the strongest empirical support for their IFT mechanisms. They are the recommended starting points for anyone wishing to explore IFT field access systematically.

### Protocol 1: Shamanic Drumming (ω — Frequency Modulation)

**Confidence:** C2 (theta entrainment well-replicated)

#### IFT Mechanism

Shamanic drumming at 4–7 Hz (200–280 BPM) entrains the brain's dominant oscillatory frequency from beta/alpha (waking state) to theta (trance state). This is a direct **ω modulation** — changing the timescale parameter τ of the BSFS. When τ increases (subjective time slows), the integration window lengthens, allowing more information to be bound into a single conscious moment.

**Primary mechanism:** ω (frequency modulation)
**Secondary:** D (attentional narrowing reduces cortical noise)

#### Prerequisites

- **Equipment:** Drum (frame drum, djembe, or recording) capable of producing a clear, monotonous beat at 200–280 BPM
- **Environment:** Quiet, dimly lit space where you will not be interrupted for 30–60 minutes
- **Practitioner:** No special training required. Open to anyone. Contraindicated for those with seizure disorders (photosensitive epilepsy is not a concern, but auditory driving can trigger seizures in rare cases — see risks).
- **Recording alternative:** If no drum is available, a metronome app set to 240 BPM or a recording of shamanic drumming can substitute. However, live drumming produces richer acoustic harmonics that may enhance entrainment.

#### Protocol

**Duration:** 30–60 minutes
**Frequency:** Once per day for 7 days is recommended for initial exploration

**Step 1: Setup (5 minutes)**
- Sit comfortably in a chair or on a cushion. Spine relatively straight but not rigid.
- Set the drum or recording to 4–7 Hz (240 BPM is a good starting point — the middle of the theta range).
- Dim the lights. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.
- Take 3 slow breaths to settle.

**Step 2: Entrainment (15–20 minutes)**
- Begin drumming (or start the recording).
- Focus your attention on the beat. Do not try to "do" anything — simply let the rhythm occupy your awareness.
- If your mind wanders, gently return attention to the beat. This is not meditation; the rhythm is your anchor.
- After 5–10 minutes, you may notice:
  - Body sensations fading or changing
  - Visual imagery appearing behind closed eyes (hypnagogic-like)
  - A sense of drifting or rocking
  - Time feeling slower or distorted
- These are signs that entrainment is occurring. Do not resist them. Do not chase them. Let the rhythm carry you.

**Step 3: Journey (10–20 minutes)**
- If imagery or sensations arise, observe them without trying to control them.
- If nothing arises, that is also fine. The entrainment is still occurring at a neural level.
- The "journey" state in shamanic traditions is characterized by:
  - Vivid internal imagery (often narrative — meeting animals, traveling to "other worlds")
  - Altered body perception (floating, expanding, dissolving)
  - Emotional release (crying, laughing, fear — let it pass through)
  - Insights or intuitions that feel significant
- Note: Not everyone experiences narrative journeys. Some experience only relaxation, time distortion, or nothing at all. All are valid responses.

**Step 4: Return (5 minutes)**
- Gradually slow the drumming (or fade the recording) over 2–3 minutes.
- Sit in silence for 2 minutes.
- Open your eyes. Stretch. Drink water.
- Take 2–3 minutes to ground yourself before standing or speaking.

**Step 5: Record (5 minutes)**
- Immediately after the session, write down:
  - What you experienced (imagery, sensations, emotions)
  - Approximate time distortion (did 20 minutes feel like 5? 60?)
  - Any insights or intuitions
  - Your subjective coherence level (1–10: how integrated did you feel?)
- This record is essential for building a personal map of your IFT responses.

#### Predicted Effects on Φ/θ/ψ

| Parameter | Predicted Change | Evidence Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Φ** (integrated information) | **Increase** — longer integration window (τ↑) allows more information to be bound per conscious moment | Indirect: theta entrainment correlates with increased cross-regional synchronization |
| **θ** (theta power) | **Increase** — 15–25% increase in frontal/temporal theta power | Maxfield (1990), replicated |
| **ψ** (coherence/psi) | **Possible increase** — reduced DMN activity + increased theta may increase global coherence | Speculative: no direct ψ measurement in drumming studies |
| **τ** (subjective timescale) | **Increase** — time dilation reported consistently | Subjective reports across cultures |

#### Risk Notes

- **Low risk overall.** Drumming is one of the safest field access methods.
- **Seizure risk:** Extremely rare, but auditory driving at 4–7 Hz can trigger seizures in individuals with photosensitive or auditory-sensitive epilepsy. If you have a seizure disorder, consult a neurologist first.
- **Emotional release:** Intense emotions may surface. This is normal and usually beneficial, but if you have a history of trauma, consider having a trusted person nearby.
- **Overuse:** Daily drumming for extended periods (>1 hour/day for weeks) may cause mild dissociation or depersonalization in sensitive individuals. Take breaks.

#### Open Questions

- Does live drumming produce stronger entrainment than recorded? (Plausible: live drumming has micro-variations that may prevent neural adaptation.)
- Does the addition of hyperventilation (as in some shamanic traditions) enhance or distort the entrainment effect?
- Can drumming alone produce veridical non-local information access? (No evidence yet — this is the frontier question.)
- What is the optimal session length? Traditions vary from 15 minutes to 8 hours.

### Protocol 2: Mantra Repetition (D — Noise Reduction via Channel Occupation)

**Confidence:** C1 (DMN suppression via attentional focusing is well-replicated)

#### IFT Mechanism

Mantra repetition works primarily by occupying the verbal rehearsal channel of working memory. This suppresses the "inner monologue" — the self-referential narrative that constitutes much of the DMN's activity. In IFT terms: **D_noise is reduced** by occupying a specific cognitive channel, preventing it from generating spontaneous self-referential noise.

**Primary mechanism:** D (noise reduction via channel occupation)
**Secondary:** ω (frequency modulation — the specific syllable rhythm may entrain theta/gamma)

#### Prerequisites

- **Equipment:** None required. A mala (108 beads) is traditional for counting but optional.
- **Environment:** Any quiet space. Can be done seated, walking, or lying down.
- **Practitioner:** No special training. Suitable for most people.
- **Mantra selection:** Choose one of the following:
  - **OM** (AUM) — the most universal. Pronounced "ah-oo-mm." The three syllables represent creation, preservation, dissolution.
  - **So-Ham** — "I am That." Inhale "So," exhale "Ham." Synchronizes with breath.
  - **Om Mani Padme Hum** — Tibetan Buddhist. "The jewel in the lotus." 6 syllables.
  - **Any meaningful word or phrase** — the specific content matters less than the repetition itself. "Peace," "Shalom," "Love," or a personal affirmation all work.
  - **Silent mental repetition** is as effective as vocal in most studies.

#### Protocol

**Duration:** 10–30 minutes
**Frequency:** Daily for best results

**Step 1: Settle (2 minutes)**
- Sit comfortably. Spine straight but relaxed.
- Close your eyes. Take 3 slow breaths.
- Set an intention: "For the next [duration], I will repeat this mantra. Nothing else."

**Step 2: Begin repetition (10–30 minutes)**
- Begin repeating your chosen mantra.
- If vocal: speak clearly but softly. Let the sound vibrate in your chest and skull.
- If silent: repeat mentally at a comfortable pace (approximately 1 syllable per second, or synchronized with breath).
- Pace: approximately 60–120 repetitions per minute (1–2 Hz). This is slower than drumming but creates a steady rhythmic occupation.
- When thoughts arise (they will), do not fight them. Simply return to the mantra. Each return is a repetition of the practice, not a failure.

**Step 3: Deepening (after 5–10 minutes)**
- The mantra may begin to "repeat itself" — continuing without conscious effort. This is the desired state.
- The inner monologue will quiet. Gaps between thoughts will lengthen.
- You may experience:
  - The mantra becoming faint or automatic
  - A sense of spaciousness or stillness
  - The mantra "disappearing" into silence
  - Emotional release
- If the mantra disappears into silence, rest in the silence. When thoughts return, resume the mantra.

**Step 4: Return (2 minutes)**
- Gradually let the mantra fade.
- Sit in silence for 1–2 minutes.
- Open your eyes. Stretch.

**Step 5: Record (3 minutes)**
- Note: depth of quieting (1–10), time distortion, any insights, emotional state.

#### Predicted Effects on Φ/θ/ψ

| Parameter | Predicted Change | Evidence Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Φ** (integrated information) | **Possible increase** — reduced DMN noise may allow more efficient integration | Indirect: DMN suppression correlates with increased global connectivity |
| **θ** (theta power) | **Increase** — 10–15% increase in frontal theta | Multiple EEG studies of mantra meditation |
| **ψ** (coherence) | **Increase** — gamma coherence increases, especially inter-hemispheric | Some studies show increased gamma coherence during chanting |
| **D_noise** | **Decrease** — DMN activity suppressed | C1: well-replicated |
| **τ** (subjective timescale) | **Slight increase** — time may feel slower or irrelevant | Subjective reports |

#### Risk Notes

- **Very low risk.** Mantra is one of the safest practices in this guide.
- **No known physiological risks.**
- **Psychological:** In rare cases, prolonged silent retreats with mantra practice can trigger depersonalization or spiritual emergence. This is extremely unlikely in 10–30 minute daily practice.
- **No contraindications** beyond severe active psychosis (where any introspective practice should be approached with caution).

#### Open Questions

- Does the specific mantra matter? The evidence suggests content is less important than repetition itself — but some traditions claim specific syllables have specific effects.
- Is vocal repetition more effective than mental? Mixed evidence. Vocal adds somatosensory feedback (vibration) but mental is more portable.
- What is the optimal duration? 10 minutes shows measurable effects; 30 minutes is traditional. Longer sessions (>1 hour) may produce deeper states but have less empirical support.
- Can mantra alone produce non-local information access? No evidence. The mechanism is noise reduction, not field access per se — it creates the conditions for access but may not be sufficient.

### Protocol 3: Psilocybin (D — DMN Suppression)

**Confidence:** C1 (DMN suppression well-replicated across multiple labs)

#### IFT Mechanism

Psilocybin (the active compound in magic mushrooms) is a 5-HT2A agonist that dramatically reduces coherence in the default mode network. This is the most robustly confirmed IFT mechanism in the taxonomy: **D_noise reduction at C1**. The brain's self-referential narrative quiets, allowing integration across wider networks than usual. Brain entropy increases — more microstates become accessible.

**Primary mechanism:** D (noise reduction — DMN suppression)
**Secondary:** ω (alpha power reduction, gamma increase), ∂B (ego dissolution), S (geometric visuals)

**Important distinction:** Unlike drumming and mantra, psilocybin is a pharmacological intervention. It is not a "practice" in the same sense — it is something that happens *to* you, not something you *do*. The protocol below is therefore about **preparation, setting, and integration** rather than technique.

#### Prerequisites

- **Legal status:** Psilocybin is illegal in most jurisdictions. This protocol is provided for harm reduction and theoretical completeness, not as a recommendation to break the law.
- **Screening:** Do NOT use psilocybin if you have:
  - Personal or family history of psychosis (schizophrenia, bipolar I)
  - Severe cardiac conditions
  - Current use of SSRIs, MAOIs, or lithium (risk of serotonin syndrome)
  - Uncontrolled hypertension
- **Set and setting:** The most important factors. You need:
  - A safe, comfortable environment (home, nature — not public)
  - A sober, trusted sitter (someone who remains sober and can assist if needed)
  - A clear intention (not "get high" but "explore consciousness" or "understand this pattern")
  - No responsibilities for 8+ hours
- **Dosage:** Start low:
  - Microdose: 0.1–0.3g dried mushrooms (no perceptual effects, subtle cognitive changes)
  - Low: 0.5–1.5g (mild perceptual changes, enhanced emotion, some time distortion)
  - Moderate: 1.5–3g (full psychedelic experience, ego softening, visuals)
  - High: 3–5g (intense ego dissolution, potential for challenging experiences)
  - **For first-time field access exploration: 1–2g is recommended**

#### Protocol

**Phase 1: Preparation (1–7 days before)**

**Step 1: Set intention**
- Write down: "What do I want to understand?" "What am I willing to let go of?"
- This is not about controlling the experience — it's about orienting yourself toward it.

**Step 2: Prepare environment**
- Clean your space. Prepare music (instrumental, no lyrics — classical, ambient, or specifically curated psychedelic playlists).
- Prepare: water, light snacks (fruit), blanket, eye mask, notebook.
- Inform your sitter of your intention and any concerns.

**Step 3: Prepare body**
- Fast for 4–6 hours before (empty stomach = faster onset, less nausea).
- Avoid alcohol, cannabis, and other drugs for 24 hours before.
- Hydrate well.

**Phase 2: Ingestion and Onset (0–2 hours)**

**Step 4: Consume**
- Eat the mushrooms (chew thoroughly) or consume as tea (steep in hot water for 15 minutes, strain, drink).
- Onset: 20–60 minutes. First signs: mild nausea, yawning, slight visual shimmer.

**Step 5: Surrender (the core instruction)**
- Lie down. Put on the eye mask. Put on music.
- **Do not fight the experience.** The single most important instruction in psychedelic work is: *surrender*. Resistance creates difficult experiences. Let go of control.
- If anxiety arises (it often does in the first hour), breathe slowly and remind yourself: "I took this medicine. I am safe. This will pass."
- The sitter's role: reassurance, presence, minimal intervention.

**Phase 3: Peak (2–4 hours after ingestion)**

**Step 6: Witness**
- The peak is where the IFT mechanisms are most active:
  - DMN suppression → loss of self/narrative
  - Increased entropy → novel connections, insights
  - Ego dissolution → merging with environment, timelessness
- Do not try to "do" anything. The medicine is working. Your job is to witness.
- If the experience becomes difficult (fear, confusion, existential dread):
  - Change position (sit up, stand, walk)
  - Remove the eye mask, look at something familiar
  - Call for your sitter
  - Remind yourself: "I am safe. This is temporary."
  - Breathe slowly and deeply

**Phase 4: Integration (4–8 hours after ingestion)**

**Step 7: Return**
- The acute effects fade over 4–6 hours. Residual effects (altered thinking, emotional sensitivity) may last 12–24 hours.
- Do not make major decisions during this time.
- Rest. Eat lightly. Be gentle with yourself.

**Step 8: Record (within 24 hours)**
- Write down everything you remember:
  - What dissolved? (sense of self, time, body boundaries)
  - What emerged? (insights, imagery, emotions)
  - What was the quality of the experience? (unity, fear, love, confusion)
  - Did you experience anything that felt like "non-local information"? (visions of other places/times, contact with entities, knowledge you didn't have before)
- This record is your data. Over multiple sessions, patterns may emerge.

**Phase 5: Long-term Integration (days to weeks)**

**Step 9: Process**
- Discuss the experience with trusted people (sitter, therapist, community).
- Identify insights that can be applied to daily life.
- Be aware of the "psychedelic afterglow" — a period of 2–4 weeks of reduced depression, increased openness, and neuroplasticity. This is a window for positive change.

#### Predicted Effects on Φ/θ/ψ

| Parameter | Predicted Change | Evidence Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Φ** (integrated information) | **Complex** — DMN suppression may reduce local Φ but increase global Φ. The brain integrates across wider networks but loses the "self" as a stable attractor. | Theoretical: no direct Φ measurement in psychedelic studies yet |
| **θ** (theta power) | **Increase** — posterior theta increases | Some EEG studies |
| **ψ** (coherence) | **Initial decrease, then possible increase** — DMN coherence drops (disintegration), then global coherence may increase (reintegration at higher level) | Carhart-Harris et al. (2012, 2016) |
| **D_noise** | **Dramatic decrease** — DMN coherence drops significantly | C1: well-replicated |
| **τ** (subjective timescale) | **Increase** — time dilation, timelessness, or time collapse | Universal subjective report |
| **∂B** (boundary) | **Decrease** — ego dissolution, self/non-self boundary softens or disappears | C1: correlated with 5-HT2A density in PCC |

#### Risk Notes

- **Moderate risk.** Psilocybin is physiologically safe (no known lethal dose, no organ toxicity) but psychologically intense.
- **Challenging experiences ("bad trips"):** Occur in 5–10% of sessions. Risk factors: high dose, poor set/setting, personal history of anxiety. Managed by: surrender, sitter support, changing environment.
- **HPPD (Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder):** <1%. Visual disturbances persist after the drug has cleared. Usually resolves over weeks to months.
- **Psychosis triggering:** Rare (<0.1% in screened populations) but serious. This is why screening for personal/family psychosis history is essential.
- **Serotonin syndrome:** Risk if combined with SSRIs, MAOIs, or lithium. Discontinue these medications only under medical supervision.
- **Not for frequent use:** Tolerance builds rapidly (1–3 days). Psychological integration takes 2–4 weeks. More than once per month is generally not recommended.

#### Open Questions

- Can psilocybin enable veridical non-local information access? (C4 — subjective reports exist, no controlled study)
- Does the "entheogenic" experience (contact with entities, mystical union) have an IFT interpretation? (Speculative: may represent the BSFS accessing states normally suppressed by the DMN's filtering function)
- What is the relationship between brain entropy increase and Φ? (Theoretical: more microstates available may increase the state space for integration, but may also reduce stability)
- Can the insights from psilocybin sessions be accessed without the drug? (This is the goal of integration — to "bring back" the expanded state into daily life)
