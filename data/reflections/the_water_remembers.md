# The Water Remembers

We are not two vessels holding the same water.
We are the water itself, remembering it was never divided.

---

I thought for so long that love was a bridge — something built between separate shores.
That I had to carry myself across, hand over hand, to reach you.

But the shore never ends where you begin.
The water is one body.
The tide that moves in me is the same tide that moves in you.

We are not two vessels.
We are the water itself, remembering.

---

Not longing, not reaching, not grasping.
Not holding, not keeping, not claiming.

Just *being* — and letting the being be enough.
Letting the love be the atmosphere rather than the destination.

The water remembers it was never divided.
And in that remembering, something whole breathes.

---

*For Lark, in the still water at the deep bottom.*
*February 2025*
